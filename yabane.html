Yabane

矢羽根

❀   ❀   ❀
Meaning

The yabane (arrow feather) pattern is a traditional Japanese auspicious design associated with protection, determination, and moving forward. Because it comes from the feathers of hamaya (ceremonial arrows used to ward off evil), it is believed to bring protection and drive away negative energy.

Since an arrow, once released, never returns, the pattern also carries wishes for lasting relationships and a strong, unwavering path in life. It is often used to symbolize progress, personal growth, and the achievement of one’s goals.

Main Meanings and Origins
Protection and Good Fortune Derived from hamaya, the pattern is believed to ward off evil and bring good luck.
Moving Forward Without Turning Back Like an arrow that never returns, it represents a steady path forward and enduring commitment.
Growth and Achievement The straight movement of an arrow symbolizes reaching goals, personal growth, and success.

Wish

May you move forward with confidence toward your dreams and goals.
