Tonbo

とんぼ

✦   ✦   ✦
Meaning

The dragonfly pattern is a traditional Japanese motif associated with victory and determination. Because dragonflies never move backward, they were admired by samurai during the Edo period and were called kachimushi ("victory insect"), symbolizing courage and success.

During the Sengoku period, warlords used dragonfly designs on helmets and armor. The dragonfly is also seen as a symbol of a good harvest, as it appears in autumn and helps protect crops by eating harmful insects. Even today, this pattern is widely used in yukata, kimono, and traditional leather crafts.

Kachimushi — Spirit of Never Retreating
Since dragonflies only fly forward, samurai saw them as a symbol of victory and bravery.

Symbol of a Good Harvest
Dragonflies protect crops in autumn, making them a sign of abundance.

Symbol of Japan
In ancient times, Japan was called Akitsushima ("Dragonfly Island"), making the dragonfly a beloved symbol of the country.

Wish
