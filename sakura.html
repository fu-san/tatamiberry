Sakura

桜

❀   ❀   ❀
Meaning

The sakura (cherry blossom) is one of the most beloved symbols in Japan and a traditional auspicious motif. It represents new beginnings, prosperity, and a bountiful harvest.

Because sakura blooms after the cold winter and has long been associated with the presence of agricultural deities, it is often used to celebrate important milestones such as weddings, graduations, and new chapters in life.

Main Meanings and Origins
Harvest and Prosperity Sakura has long been linked to agricultural beliefs, where it was seen as a sacred tree inhabited by the spirit of the rice field deity, symbolizing a good harvest.
New Beginnings and Good Fortune Blooming in spring, it represents fresh starts, new journeys, and positive beginnings.
Beauty and Transience Its delicate blossoms and brief bloom reflect a sense of beauty, as well as the fleeting nature of life.
