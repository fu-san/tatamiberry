Daruma

だるま

❀   ❀   ❀
Meaning

The Daruma is a traditional Japanese auspicious motif associated with good fortune. Its design is rich in symbolic elements: the eyebrows represent cranes, and the mustache and beard represent turtles, both of which symbolize longevity and prosperity.

The golden pattern on the body represents a monk's robe (kesa), while the red color is believed to ward off evil and protect the household. The round shape of the Daruma expresses the spirit of "fall down seven times, stand up eight," symbolizing perseverance and resilience.

Wish

May your efforts lead you to success, and may you always find the strength to stand up again.
