Shippo

七宝

❀   ❀   ❀
Meaning

The Shippo (seven treasures) pattern is a traditional Japanese auspicious design symbolizing harmony, peace, and endless connections. Its interlocking circles extend infinitely, representing the idea of relationships and bonds that continue without end, as well as wishes for prosperity and the growth of future generations.

The name “Shippo” comes from the seven treasures described in Buddhist teachings—gold, silver, lapis lazuli, crystal, coral, agate, and giant clam shell—suggesting that human connections are as valuable as these precious treasures.

Main Meanings and Origins
Harmony and Connection The pattern is created by overlapping equal-sized circles, forming an endless chain that symbolizes rich relationships and meaningful connections.
Wealth and Prosperity The “seven treasures” refer to valuable materials in Buddhist texts, representing wishes for fortune and abundance.
Family Growth and Continuity The infinite circle pattern reflects the idea of generations continuing and flourishing.
Origin of the Name It is said that the name evolved from the word “shihō” (meaning “in all directions”), as the circles expand outward endlessly.
