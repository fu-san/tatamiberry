Kiku

菊

❀   ❀   ❀
Meaning

The kiku (chrysanthemum) pattern is one of Japan’s most prestigious auspicious designs. It symbolizes longevity, good health, and protection from evil, as well as a sense of nobility and inner balance.

Because of its elegant and timeless nature, it is used throughout the year in kimono, obi, and traditional ceramics such as Imari ware.

Main Meanings and Origins
Longevity and Good Health The chrysanthemum has long been associated with long life and well-being.
Protection and Purification It is believed to ward off negative energy and bring a sense of clarity and calm.
Nobility and Harmony Its refined form represents dignity, stability, and balance of mind and body.

Wish

May happiness continue in your life and your heart be always fulfilled.
